About

Lingxing Yao is an Associate Professor in the Department of Mathematics at The University of Akron. His work connects mathematical models, numerical algorithms, and simulations with questions from biology and material science.

His research has included osmotic water flow and cell migration, chemo-mechanical oscillations in polyelectrolyte hydrogels, chemical transport and reaction in cellular suspensions, and computational methods for moving interfaces and complex fluids.

Research

Cell motility

Models for cell migration driven by actin recycling and osmotic water flow, with emphasis on confined environments, energetic efficiency, and membrane-fluid interaction.

Polyelectrolyte hydrogels

Mathematical analysis and numerical simulation of pH-sensitive hydrogel membranes for rhythmic drug delivery and chemo-mechanical oscillation.

Numerical algorithms and simulation

Numerical algorithms for fluid-structure interaction, solute transport, diffusion, reaction, and moving membrane boundaries in two spatial dimensions.

Complex fluids and materials

Mathematical modeling and computation for liquid crystal polymers, viscoelastic materials, micro-bead diffusion, and related material systems.
